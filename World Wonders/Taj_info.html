About Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum in India, built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. It stands as a symbol of love and is considered one of the most beautiful buildings in the world.

Facts

  • Construction Period: Built between 1632 and 1653.
  • Location: Agra, India, on the banks of the Yamuna River.
  • UNESCO Status: Recognized as a World Heritage Site in 1983.
  • Material Used: White marble sourced from Makrana, Rajasthan.
  • Unique Feature: Changes color depending on the time of day, appearing pinkish at sunrise, white in the afternoon, and golden at sunset.
  • Architect: Designed by Ustad Ahmad Lahauri.
  • Intricate Artwork: Features floral patterns, calligraphy, and detailed inlay work with precious stones.

Significance

The Taj Mahal is not only an architectural marvel but also a testament to the rich history and craftsmanship of the Mughal era. It attracts millions of visitors each year and remains one of the world's most visited tourist destinations.

Interesting Facts

  • More than 20,000 artisans were involved in its construction.
  • The calligraphy on the entrance gates contains verses from the Quran.
  • The minarets surrounding the Taj Mahal are slightly tilted outward to protect the main structure from earthquakes.
  • It is estimated that over 1,000 elephants were used to transport materials.
